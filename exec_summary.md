
Executive Summary

Industrial organizations depend on reliable equipment performance to maintain productivity and control operational costs. However, many maintenance operations still rely on reactive approaches, where repairs occur only after equipment failure. This often results in unplanned downtime, higher repair costs, and inefficient use of resources.

This project demonstrates how a modern analytics engineering platform can support predictive maintenance by integrating operational sensor data with maintenance and asset management systems.

Using BigQuery as the data warehouse and dbt for transformation, the platform consolidates IoT telemetry, maintenance records, and asset metadata into structured analytics datasets. These datasets enable organizations to monitor equipment performance, identify potential failure patterns, and track key reliability metrics such as Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF) and Mean Time To Repair (MTTR).

Power BI dashboards provide decision-makers with visibility into asset health, downtime trends, maintenance efficiency, and the financial impact of equipment failures. By transforming fragmented operational data into reliable analytics models, the platform enables organizations to move from reactive maintenance toward proactive and data-driven asset management.

Ultimately, the solution demonstrates how analytics engineering can help industrial companies reduce downtime, optimize maintenance planning, and improve overall operational reliability.
